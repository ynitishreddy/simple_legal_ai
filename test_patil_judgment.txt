JUDGMENT
(Per Hon'ble Justice A.M. Khanwilkar)

1. The present appeal arises from the judgment and order dated 3rd August 2023 passed by the learned Additional Sessions Judge, Pune, in Sessions Case No. 87 of 2022, whereby the appellant was convicted under Section 302 of the Indian Penal Code and sentenced to imprisonment for life.

2. The prosecution case, in brief, is as follows. On the night of 14th August 2022, the deceased Suresh Patil, aged 48 years, was found murdered in his residence at Kothrud, Pune. The First Information Report (FIR) was lodged by the deceased's wife, Smt. Kavita Patil, at Kothrud Police Station at 11:45 PM on 14th August 2022. The appellant, a distant cousin of the deceased, was named as the prime suspect.

3. The investigating officer, PI Arun Shelar, took up the investigation and arrested the appellant Ramesh Vinayak Patil on 16th August 2022, i.e., two days after the murder. The arrest was effected from the appellant's residence at Wakad, Pune, pursuant to a tip-off received by the police.

4. On 19th August 2022, the appellant was produced before the learned Judicial Magistrate First Class, Pune, and was remanded to police custody for a period of five days. Three days following the arrest, on 19th August 2022, the appellant is alleged to have led police to a dry well behind Wakad village, where the murder weapon — a blood-stained wooden baton — was recovered.

5. The bail application filed on behalf of the appellant was taken up for hearing on 25th August 2022. On that date, after hearing both sides at length, the learned JMFC rejected the bail application on the grounds that the offence was grave and the accused was a flight risk. A subsequent bail application was filed before the Sessions Court on 10th September 2022, which was also rejected on 18th September 2022.

6. The charge-sheet was filed by the investigating agency on 14th November 2022, within the stipulated period. Prior to the filing of the charge-sheet, the forensic science laboratory (FSL) report was received by the investigating officer on 2nd November 2022, confirming that the blood found on the recovered baton matched the DNA profile of the deceased.

7. The Sessions Trial commenced on 15th February 2023. Twenty-three prosecution witnesses were examined between 15th February 2023 and 20th July 2023. The defence examined two witnesses. The learned Additional Sessions Judge, after appreciating the evidence, returned a finding of guilt against the appellant and, by the impugned judgment dated 3rd August 2023, convicted and sentenced him as aforesaid.

8. Having heard the learned counsel for the parties and having gone through the record and proceedings, we are of the opinion that the prosecution has proved its case beyond reasonable doubt. The chain of circumstances — the motive of property dispute, the recovery of the weapon at the instance of the accused, the FSL report, and the testimony of eyewitnesses — forms an unbroken chain pointing unerringly to the guilt of the accused.

9. For the foregoing reasons, the appeal is dismissed. The conviction and sentence recorded by the trial court are confirmed.

(Justice A.M. Khanwilkar)
(Justice S.P. Tavade)

Mumbai, 18th September 2023.
